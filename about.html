Our History

Dr. Benedict Urmaza did not have to look far for his inspiration to be an ophthalmologist. His father, Dr. Santiago Urmaza, built an extensive reputation as a pre-eminent ophthalmologist on Long Island, known for his rapport with patients and his surgical skills. Dr. Santiago Urmaza began the practice in 1978 in Patchogue. When the practice outgrew the space, he moved to the present office at 887 Church Street, Bohemia. While he took care of his patients at St. Lucy’s, Dr. Santiago Urmaza also periodically returned to his native Philippines to perform restorative eye surgery to the indigent population. On several of these volunteer-based medical missions, Dr. Ben Urmaza would accompany and assist his father as a medical student, then as a board-certified ophthalmologist. Dr. Ben Urmaza took over St. Lucy’s Eye Care in 2001, allowing his father to retire and fulfill his dream of opening an eye care hospital in the Philippines. Dr. Ben Urmaza has continued his father’s tradition of individualized patient care, with his cataract surgical skills and a focus on thoroughness of eye exams and educating patients on their medical eye concerns.

St. Lucy, Patron Saint of Vision

Saint Lucy, also known as Lucia of Syracuse (284–304 A.D.), was an early Christian who was martyred during the Diocletianic Persecution in the Roman Empire. She is one of the most highly venerated saints in Christianity and one of only eight women mentioned by name in the Roman Catholic Mass. Accounts of her life differ, but most religious scholars agree that she was martyred after a disappointed suitor reported her as a Christian to Roman authorities.

What Makes Us Different

Because St. Lucy's Eye Care is a solo independent practice, Dr. Urmaza chooses to take the time necessary to care for his patients,"like how traditional medicine was practiced," as one patient has said. But there is nothing "old-school" about the technologies Dr. Urmaza uses for his patients. He maintains modern diagnostic machines and keeps current on his continuing medical education (CME).

The patient will be treated only by one practitioner, Dr. Urmaza, for eye exams, refractions, diagnostic imaging and tests, procedures, and surgeries so the patient can be assured of consistent quality results and interpretations. A patient being seen by one provider is less exposed to COVID19 and other communicable diseases.

We purposely schedule our patients in a manner so that they will not arrive to a crowded waiting room and parking is plentiful. This protects our patients, staff, and our doctor. Since doctor visits can be stressful already, we hope to provide a calm environment for patients to come to for their eye care.
